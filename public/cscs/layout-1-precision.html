PASC 26 — 2026  ·  13th Annual Conference

Building Trust in Science
through HPC Co-Design

An interdisciplinary platform for the exchange of knowledge in scientific computing, with a focus on methods, algorithms, and application challenges in the context of high-performance computing.

Dates June 29 – July 1, 2026
Location Bern, Switzerland
Venue University of Bern & PHBern

Conference Theme

Key Research Areas

PASC 26 addresses the pressing question: how can we ensure trust in the methods, processes, and solutions derived through exascale-class co-design?

AI in Medicine & Healthcare

Enhancing confidence and reliability in computational diagnoses and medical simulations through rigorous co-design methodologies.

Extreme Weather Event Modelling

Demonstrating model validity and reproducibility for climate simulation at exascale, enabling trusted environmental forecasts.

Energy Assurance & Security

The role of HPC co-design in reliable, resilient energy infrastructure modelling and grid-scale simulation.

Epidemiological Modelling

Trustworthy simulation methodologies for public health decision-making and disease modelling at population scale.

Digital Twins & Virtual Environments

Enabling verifiable representations of complex physical systems, from cardiovascular engineering to aerospace dynamics.

Reproducibility & Open Science

Best practices for validated, reproducible, and reliable scientific results in HPC contexts using open data and software artifacts.
