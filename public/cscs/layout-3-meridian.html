13th Edition  ·  PASC 26

Building Trust in Science
through HPC Co-Design

An interdisciplinary conference for the exchange of knowledge in scientific computing, with focus on methods, algorithms, and application challenges at the exascale frontier.

Dates June 29 – July 1, 2026
Location Bern, Switzerland
Register Now View Programme
How do we build confidence that our computations are correct, that our results can be trusted, and that they make a meaningful difference in the world?
PASC 26 Scientific Theme — Building Trust in Science through HPC Co-Design

The 2026 edition explores trust across five pillars: AI in Medicine, Extreme Weather Modelling, Energy Assurance, Epidemiological Modelling, and Digital Twins.

500+Participants
30+Countries
3Days
7Scientific Domains
Research Areas

Key Scientific Domains

PASC 26 spans seven scientific domains, reflecting the breadth of modern computational science.

AI in Medicine & Healthcare

Confidence and reliability in computational diagnoses and medical simulations through rigorous co-design methodologies.

Extreme Weather Event Modelling

Demonstrating model validity and reproducibility for climate simulation at exascale.

Energy Assurance & Security

HPC co-design for reliable, resilient energy infrastructure modelling and grid-scale simulation.

Epidemiological Modelling

Trustworthy simulation for public health decision-making and disease modelling at population scale.

Digital Twins & Virtual Environments

Verifiable representations of complex physical systems, from cardiovascular engineering to aerospace dynamics.

Chemistry & Materials Science

Ab initio and molecular dynamics simulations at scale, bridging quantum mechanics and material properties.
